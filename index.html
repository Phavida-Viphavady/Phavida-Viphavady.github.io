About Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum

❮ ❯

Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum , located in Boston, Massachusetts, United States, is home to a varied collection of paintings, sculptures, decorations, books, and historical objects from different sources around the world.

The museum was named after the founder, Isabella Stewart Gardner.Inspired by Palazzo Barboro in Venice, Gardner started her construction plan of the building in 1899.

In 1903, Gardner opened the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum as a place to display her art collection which she acquired from her trips to Europe, Russia, Egypt, The Middle East, and Asia.

Before her death in 1924, Gardner stated on her will that her art collection in the museum should remain the same, and no items should be acquired or sold.

Unfortunately, on the 18th of March, 1990. The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum was robbed by two thieves. They stole 13 valuable pieces of art which include well-known pieces from Veneer and Rembrandt with a total value of $500 million.

Since then the FBI has investigated the case and the museum also offers $10 million for information about the stolen pieces. However, the case remains unsolved until this day and has been regarded as the greatest art heist in history.
