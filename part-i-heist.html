Part I: The Heist

According to written record and the data from the motion detectors , two security guards were working at the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum on the night of the robbery on the 18th of March 1990. That night, two men in police uniform intruded the museum and stole 13 artworks worth $500 Million in 81 minutes.

The timeline of the heist is displayed below.

1:24 AM

Two white men disguised in Boston police uniform with moustaches and glasses rang the bell of one of the museum entrances and demanded to be let in to check the museum for disturbance. Rick Abath, one of the night guards at the watch desk, decided to break the museum's protocol and let the two men in. When the men were inside, they ordered Rick to call another guard who was doing a round. Then the men handcuffed them, wrapped their eyes, ears, and mouths with tape, and locked them in the basement.

Original Photo: Police Men

1:48 AM - 2:28 AM

After constraining the security guards, they turned off security cameras as well as a central computer. During this time, the thieves were roaming back and forth between the Short Gallery, the Dutch Room, and the Blue Room. With knives, chisels, and screwdrivers, they removed several valuable canvases from the frame; they also snatched some displayed art and decoration in these rooms.

In total, the thieves were able to steal 13 pieces of art which include:

  • 5 Degas and an Eagle Finial from the Short Gallery.
  • 3 Rembrandt, a Vermeer, a Flinck, and a Chinese bronze from the Dutch Room.
  • A Manet from the Blue Room.

Original Photo: Motion Detector Record

2:40 AM - 2:45 AM

The motion detectors detected that the inside and outside doors were opened twice. This showed that the thieves were leaving the museum separately within 5 minutes. They also destroyed the evidence from security footage and printed record from the motion detector before they left.

Original Photo: Netflix: This Is a Robbery

7:00 AM - 7:20 AM

A worker arrived in front of the museum and rang the doorbell but no one replied. 20 minutes later A female guard came to the museum to replace the two night guards. When she arrived, the worker informed her that no one was responding. Worried, they called their supervisor.

Original Photo: The Security Guard

7:40 AM

The supervisor arrived and used a passkey to enter the building to see the disruption in the museum. Searching the whole building they were able to help the securities guards. Unfortunately, they also discover several many frames left empty on the floor without the paintings which indicated that the museum was robbed.

The pieces were missed since and have yet returned.
